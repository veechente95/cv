About Me

I was born and raised in East Los Angeles, California more specifically a community called Boyle Heights. I completed my undergraduate studies at UCLA in 2018 majoring in Geography and minoring in Geographic Information Systems and Technology (GIS&T) and currently enrolled in python developer bootcamps. On my free time I love to cook, produce music and workout.

My Coding Experience

For as long as I can remember, I have always been fascinated by the world of tech. Coming from a career that focuses on GIS mapping, data management non-profit organizational work, I genuinely believed I was never good enough; However, I learned to accept the vulnerability and excitement to start again, stretch my limts and teach myself computer science.

My Goal

As a first generation Mexican-American self-taught python developer, I acknowledge the lack of diversity in traditional STEM fields and want to break the stigma by providing a diverse open door for future POC and Latinx folks coming from different communities, backgrounds and technical abilities. I want to use my story to help guide others who may have also expereinced similar cases of imposter syndrome.
